Who Should File
Income Tax Return?

Whether you are an individual earning a salary or a large corporation, filing ITR is a crucial step for financial transparency and compliance.

Salaried Individuals

Mandatory if total income exceeds basic exemption limit. We help with Form 16 & HRA.

Self-Employed Professionals

Doctors, Lawyers, CAs, and Architects earning from their independent practice.

Business Owners (SMEs)

Proprietors and MSME owners earning profits from trade or manufacturing.

Freelancers & Consultants

IT consultants, designers, and digital creators working with multiple clients.

NRIs / RNORs

Individuals with foreign income or assets who need to report Indian earnings.

Investors (Stocks/Crypto)

Trading in Stocks, Mutual Funds, Crypto, or F&O requires detailed reporting.

Hindu Undivided Family

HUFs managed by a Karta, seeking to optimize tax through family units.

Revised & Belated ITR

Correct past errors or file late returns before the final deadline.

Private & Public Ltd

All companies registered in India must file ITR regardless of profit/loss.

Partnership Firms & LLPs

Firms earning income through business collaboration and professional services.

Trusts & NGOs

Societies, Trusts, and NGOs required to file under Section 139(4A/4B/4C/4D).

High-Value Transactors

Individuals with high electricity bills, foreign travel, or large bank deposits.

Which ITR Form
Applies To You?

Select a form to see who should use it.

Sahaj Form

For Individuals (Salaried)

For residents having total income up to ₹50 lakh from salaries, one house property, other sources (interest etc.), and agricultural income up to ₹5,000.

  • Income from Salary/Pension
  • One House Property
  • Income from Other Sources

For Individuals & HUF

For Individuals and HUFs not having income from profits and gains of business or profession.

  • Capital Gains (Stocks, Property)
  • Foreign Assets/Income
  • More than one house property

For Business/Profession

For individuals and HUFs having income from profits and gains of business or profession.

  • Professional Income
  • Proprietary Business
  • Audit cases
Sugam Form

Presumptive Business

For Individuals, HUFs and Firms (other than LLP) being a resident having total income up to ₹50 lakh and having income from business and profession computed under sections 44AD, 44ADA or 44AE.

For Firms/LLPs/AOPs

For persons other than- (i) individual, (ii) HUF, (iii) company and (iv) person filing Form ITR-7.

For Companies

For Companies other than companies claiming exemption under section 11.

For Trusts/NGOs

For persons including companies required to furnish return under sections 139(4A) or 139(4B) or 139(4C) or 139(4D).

Tax Deadlines &
Penalty Calendar

JUL

31st July

Due date for individual and non-audit ITR filings.

OCT

31st October

Due date for audit cases and companies.

DEC

31st December

Last date for Belated ITR with ₹5,000 penalty (Section 234F).

Penalty Structure

  • Section 234F (Late Fee) ₹1,000 for income up to ₹5L, ₹5,000 if income > ₹5L.
  • Section 234A (Interest) 1% per month on unpaid tax liability for each month of delay.
  • Loss Forfeiture Cannot carry forward business/capital losses if ITR is filed late.
